What is pinworm?

Pinworms—also known as seat worms and threadworms—are little parasites. When a person is infected with pinworms, the eggs hatch and the small, thin, white worms grow and live inside the intestine.

When someone has pinworms, it is also known as a pinworm infection.

Adult pinworms are less than one-half inch long.

While a person sleeps, female pinworms crawl out of a person’s bottom to lay thousands of eggs. The tiny, sticky pinworm eggs are laid just outside in the anal area. This can lead to intense itching and scratching.

Once a person touches pinworm eggs, usually from scratching his or her bottom, the eggs can be spread to other objects and surfaces without anyone knowing it.

Pinworm eggs are so sticky, they can cling to toys, bedding, towels, clothing, and lots of other things people share every day. That's how pinworm infection spreads so easily from person to person.

Adult pinworms are less than one-half inch long.

Did you know pinworm may infect up to 40 million people in the US?

Pinworm infects people of all ages and socioeconomic levels.

In fact, pinworm is the most common parasitic worm infection in the US—and one of the most common parasitic worm infections worldwide.

May Have Pinworm Infection
Pinworm spreads most easily among:

  • Young children between the ages of 3 to 14 years
  • Toddlers (2 to 4 years old) who put their fingers in their mouths
  • Family members and caregivers of infected persons
  • Children in schools and daycare facilities

Pinworm infection in the US
Pinworm infections happen more often than head lice infestations among young children in the US.

In fact, pinworm cases are 3 times more common than head lice cases.
