Pinworm infection spreads easily from person to person

Pinworm is spread by swallowing contagious pinworm eggs

At night, female pinworms lay sticky, tiny eggs on the skin around the infected person’s anal area.

The infected person scratches this area and doesn’t wash his or her hands, leaving pinworm eggs on fingertips and under fingernails.

When the infected person touches an object, that object becomes contaminated with pinworm eggs.

The eggs can live on these indoor surfaces for up to 3 weeks.

Another person comes along and touches that contaminated object.

Now, that person has pinworm eggs on their hands or fingers.

When that person touches his or her mouth and swallows eggs, the infection has begun.
